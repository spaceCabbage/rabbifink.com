]]> By: MarkSoFla http://finkorswim.com/2013/04/29/some-reverse-advice-from-maimonides-study-torah/#comment-12492 Tue, 30 Apr 2013 03:09:00 +0000 http://finkorswim.com/?p=7032#comment-12492 Or perhaps neither is true. At the time, people didn’t have easy access to books, I’ve read that many people had access to only one or two books in their lifetime (one of those usually being the bible, and the other almost definitely a religious book as well). So if people only had access to a tiny number of books, and that they were mostly religious in nature, it is very likely that they looked toward books in a generally very reverent manner. And if the only book you had access to were, say, a New Testament, and if you read that book over and over again (remember, it’s all you’ve got access to), and if you approached books in a reverent manner, there is a decently high possibility of being strongly influenced by said book.

On the other hand, today, with access to countless books, and libraries galore, and online access to many times that amount, we naturally treat the material less reverently, and we are much less likely to be strongly influenced by any single book, certainly not to the extent of being swayed to a different religion.

I therefore conclude that this halacha is modified in our time, and if a person is sophisticated enough and learned enough and committed to his/her faith enough, reading such material is permitted.
