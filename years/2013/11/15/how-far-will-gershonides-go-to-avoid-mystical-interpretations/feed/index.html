From the name of the River Yabok (from the root of the otherwise strange word “maabak”/wrestle) to the name Peniel, to not eating the sciatic nerve, to the confusion over how the name “Yisrael” came about (because there are two different explanations given, one that it came from the angel, and one that it came from God), to the conflicting reasons for the name “machanayim”, and then at the end of the parsha, the name “alon bachut” – the entire episode has the hallmarks of one big etiology. The widespread and obvious use of etiliology in the Bible is, in my opinion, THE biggest problem thinking orthodox Jews must confront. It is a blessing that 99% of the people go their entire lives without ever picking up on these problems. Like the brothers facing Joseph,or a man trying to find the pickles in the fridge, or the Purloined Letter of Poe, something can be right in front of your face, and yet be practically invisible.
