Regarding your critiques, the first issue you raise I think is a result of many of the chapters previously having been independent  artciles and only later brought together to form this book. Much of the beginnings and endings of each chapter in which he tells you what you’ve read and what you will read was YF’s attempt at a segue. 

In your interpretation of the R Shwab quote, you’ve really only spelled out what was written by him in dumbed down language. I don’t think whether you see that he acknowledge that history is skewed really makes a difference; either way he is in support Haredi Fan Fiction. 

I find the quote from Artscroll, about R Shraga F. M. interesting, in that R Aron Kotler is really the individual credited with introducing the concept of torah study for its own sake, or in other words, to create “soldiers” and not “generals.” Wonder how that dispute will end in a world where everything can be true…….

Regarding R YW, I don’t really know anything about him, although being the RY of NIRC (in the past) is certainly a sign that he wasn’t mainstream Haredi. However, an acquaintance of mine once said that RYW was in fact not a rationalist and used one of his sons as a benchmark. I don’t know who his children are. 

I think today’s R Aron Feldman (whose brother Emmanuel wrote one of my favorite books as a kid, ‘The Shul Without A Clock’) gives of the aura of not being Haredi, but the snippets I read from his book ‘Eye of the Storm’ seem to indicate otherwise.
