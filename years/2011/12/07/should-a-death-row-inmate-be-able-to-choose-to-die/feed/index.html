The jury has spoken and Jerry concers with their judgement.

The sanctity of life is the reason that the death penalty is invoked. When an innocent life is intentionally destroyed, the guilty party has forfieted their right to live, because the violation is so great.

All sanctions are based upon taking away that which is valued, be it money (fines), time and labor (community service), freedom (incarceration) and life (execution).

The waiving of appeals enhances the just aspect of the sanction. Rarely do criminals have the moral awareness to waive appeals. There only concern is besting the system, as much as they can, to avid as much sanction as possible. Only an honorable criminal will have the awareness to waive their appeals.

Waiving appeals is part of the proper procedure, that is why all of those convicted have the option of appealing, or not, at their discretion. The law, the courts and procedure all respect the ability of those convicted to waive appeals, as they should.

Improperly all states require the first round of death penalty appeals, but not successive ones. That should be up to the convicted party.

There can be no reason that justice should not be done.
