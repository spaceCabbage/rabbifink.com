I think that the public outrage is related to frustration over a child’s death and a mother’s seeming immorality. Here you have two stories about the suspect. If we are to believe her disgraceful alibi, she discovered her daughter drowned in a swimming pool and then tried desperately to hide the evidence. After dumping the body (or, allegedly, getting Daddy to handle it) she spent time partying away. Conversely, if you don’t believe the alibi, she murdered the child in cold blood and dumped the body herself, and THEN spent time partying away. What a delightful set of options we have to choose from.

So I think from the public point of view, she falls into one of two repugnant roles: vicious child-killer, or disgustingly self-centered narcissist. Thus, I think that the public is angry that the punishment (or lack thereof) does not seem to be appopriate for either scenario; whether she’s a killer or a pig, she won’t be getting punished in either case. And the public doesn’t like that outcome, for obvious reasons!

(I am aware that she technically could face an additional two years in prison for lying to a police officer. Assuming they don’t just give her time served. In any event.)
