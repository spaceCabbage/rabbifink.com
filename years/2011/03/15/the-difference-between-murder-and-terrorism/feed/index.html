There’s terrorism and various kinds of murder as defined in penal codes,which represent legal judgments rendered by courts of law, and then there’s the kind of judgments passed by courts of public opinion (pundits, regular joes, newspapers, etc.).

For something to legally be terrorism, it has to meet a whole bunch of specific definitions.

For something to be popularly termed terrorism, it just has to “feel” like terrorism. There doesn’t need to be a scientific definition, just like the question of whether last night’s game was a “good” game doesn’t need a scientific definition to make the determination of what “good games” are. It’s a subjective label.
