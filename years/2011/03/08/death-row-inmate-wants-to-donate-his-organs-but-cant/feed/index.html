There is no additional penalty legislated of “making him feel bad” or “denying his life purpose”. There are some inescapably unpleasant consequences of being sentenced to death, and the convict must bear them, but anything beyond that would be an unlegislated punishment. For example, it would not be legal to dress a death row convict in clothing purposely designed to be uncomfortable on the grounds that he ought to suffer. Neither would it be permissible to feed him disgusting food.

Presumably the court felt that losing the right to donate your organs (if there is such a right) is one of those inescapable consequences of being sentenced to death. I’d be curious what their reasoning is.
