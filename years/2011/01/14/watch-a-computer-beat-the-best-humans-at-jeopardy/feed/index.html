Having heard about AI for years, decades even, and knowing that it isn’t anywhere as simple as additional computing power, this is really quite impressive. Thank you for posting it!

As far as IBM’s choice of what to highlight, I think it’s very simple. If they choose, let’s say, even if the results are an impressive reduction in traffic in certain areas, the vast majority of people will say “so what?” because they still see and experience traffic there and elsewhere (regardless of whether the solution provides an impressive reduction of traffic on a percentage-wise basis). If they choose hunger, and devise a program to much better route shipments to places that their device can predict require it, there will still be plenty of areas with hunger (both due to logistics and politics/etc) and people will say “so what?” because they still see hunger around the world. But this choice is a clear and well-understood “victory” by the people who will view it.

Besides, it’s research at this point, practical application will [hopefully] come later. This particular piece of research may or may not be valuable someday, but like all research, it’s still not “useless” in that it may be part of the steps to further research that can truly improve the world.
