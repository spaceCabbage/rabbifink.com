I think it’s a symptom of the fact that many ancient Jewish sources instinctively feel wrong or unnatural to modern people. Modern Orthodox Jews have a variety of strategies for dealing with this, for example privileging certain opinions (like the Rambam’s rationalism), or saying that society has changed and some social structures must change along with it. But charedim cannot accept these strategies on principle. So the problem of unnaturalness hits them with full force, and they are so shell-shocked by it that they reach the conclusion that ANYTHING natural must be against the Torah, that the Torah SHOULD feel unnatural and arbitrary, because what we feel is “natural” is a function of our animalistic yetzer hara rather than the higher wisdom of the Torah. Just as we have to reject our intuition when it comes to Amalek or ben sorer umoreh, so, they say, we cannot trust our intuition when it comes to choosing between one halachic opinion and another.
