]]> By: Rabbi Ovadiah Bander http://finkorswim.com/2014/08/22/impressive-insight-on-suicide-from-the-aruch-hashulchan/#comment-16985 Fri, 22 Aug 2014 22:51:00 +0000 http://finkorswim.com/?p=8733#comment-16985 Rabbi Fink, just to make you aware of another very important “source” for this discussion, it also very impressive because it is an extremely halachic source as well. The Pischei Teshuvah, one of the important commentators on the Shulchan Aruch, in Yoreh Deah Siman 345 says the only time suicide is considered “meabeid atzmo l’daas” is if he does it on principle like an existentialist. Similar to the oft-quoted statement from Nietzsche that “G-d is dead” and that life is futile. But, says the Pischei Teshuvah, if someone kills themselves because of depression, anxiety, pain, or the like, that is not considered suicide. That is considered halachically as “meabeid atzmo shelo l’daas” and that is not considered willfully taking one’s life, which is what suicide is. That is considered as killing one’s self because of pain or suffering, which is (terribly unfortunate, but) understandable and called without “daas.” This is unfortunately discussed as well in the Teshuvos Mimaamakim, the questions that came out of the depths of the Holocaust to the great Rav Ephraim Oshri, the Rav of the Warsaw ghetto.
