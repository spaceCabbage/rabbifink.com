]]> By: Baruch_Gitlin http://finkorswim.com/2014/08/07/was-the-war-in-gaza-worthwhile/#comment-16883 Sun, 10 Aug 2014 08:08:00 +0000 http://finkorswim.com/?p=8676#comment-16883 I respectfully submit that “was it worth it” is the wrong question. The right question is “what other choice was there.” Hamas was gradually increasing the rate of rocket fire, and it seems clear to me – and I think to most Israelis – that they were determined to continue the rate of fire until they provoked an Israeli response. Should there have been a different response? No response? Should Israel allow a situation in which the towns and settlements around Gaza are gradually rendered uninhabitable? Would a lack of any response, or an inadequate response, encourage Hizballah to undertake a similar war of attrition in the North? Would it boost Hamas’s public standing vis-a-vis the Palestinian Authority, thereby rendering any long term peace agreement much less likely? All of these factors need to be taken into account in evaluating the war. Which is not over, as Alan Levin points out.

A more important question, though, is the following. In the past, when Israel has refused on security grounds to make concessions in the peace process that would lead to a Palestinian state, advocates of a two-state solution have argued that Israel can rely on its military strength to take risks for peace. However, we have seen in Gaza that world opinion, and perhaps our own collective concience, put significant limitations on Israel’s ability to deal with attacks from Gaza. Imagine a scenario in which a Palestinian state is formed in the West Bank/Judea and Samaria, and comes under control of Hamas or some other group acting as Hamas has been acting in Gaza. In this scenario, it wouldn’t be a handful of towns and settlements and several cities under nearly constant rocket threat – it would be most of the country. In this Gaza war, air traffic to Israel was significantly disrupted for several days due to a Hamas rocket that fell near the airport. If these attacks were coming from the West Bank, they would be able to shut down the airport completely, with ease. What implications does the reaction of the international community towards the operation in Gaza have for Israel’s ability to use rely on its military strength to alleviate these “risks for peace” in such a scenario? I cite a recent blog post by Rabbi Slifkin on this point: http://www.rationalistjudaism.com/2014/07/gaza-killed-palestine.html. The irony of all this is that Israel’s ability, or lack thereof, to deter attacks from Gaza has a direct impact on the feasibility of the two-state solution.
In short, I believe it is quite insufficient to evaluate the war in terms of lives lost, or lives that might have been lost, here and now. There are much bigger, long-term issues that have to be considered. War is terrible, we shouldn’t forget that, and we shouldn’t be oblvious to the suffering on the Palestinian side. But I don’t think this country (Israel) would exist if our leaders had only considered the short term costs/benefits in making their decisions on war and peace.
