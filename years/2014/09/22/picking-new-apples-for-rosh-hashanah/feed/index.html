If the Hebrew Wikipedia (“סדר ליל ראש השנה#מאכלים נוספים“) is anything to go by, eating apples is a Rishonic rather than a Talmudic custom:
Using honey is Geonic in origin (based on a responsum by Natronai Gaon). According to Mahzor Vitri (end of section 323) in [northern] France they ate red apples, but in Provence (southern France) they ate white grapes and white figs. Only later were the customs of honey and apple united into what we know today (and gets mention in Tur Orah Hayim 583). Later still it reached Sephardic communities (though with reservations regarding the use of honey).

So, if people are fine with not eating pumpkin, fenugreek, leek, beet or dates (based on the Soncino translation of Kerithoth 6a), which are mentioned in the Talmud, Then they should have no problem with eating some other (more accessible) kind of sweet fruit (or vegetable), instead of apples, who’s origins are Rishonic (and regional), to begin with.
