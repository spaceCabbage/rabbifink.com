]]> By: Adam http://finkorswim.com/2010/12/10/if-you-criticize-israel-youre-not-a-jew/#comment-4163 Adam Sun, 12 Dec 2010 06:23:00 +0000 http://finkorswim.com/?p=3445#comment-4163 A few Neturei Karta types beat up a Jew. That’s the exception, not the rule. From your reporting of Cohen’s article, he doesn’t point that out.

I don’t use the phrase “not a Jew” lightly. That’s a halachic distinction, not a political one.

What is important to point out, to Jew and Gentile alike, is that forming an opinion on Israel based solely on the nightly news can be very dangerous. If you don’t live here in Israel, don’t assume that you know the best course of action for us.

It reminds me of a funny story from this past summer. There was a discussion at the dining room table of friends of mine in Maale Adumim, a large suburbia, population 30,000 – about seven minutes drive from Jerusalem. A young liberal Jew sat at his hosts’ table, conversing about “the matsav” (the situation). He adamantly declared that “the settlements” are illegal, immoral, and that he would never set foot in such a place.

His hosts, trying to stifle their own laughter, calmly pointed out that he was sitting in a so-called “settlement” right now.

He young man was baffled. “What?! This can’t be a settlement!”

This college graduate had been educated by CNN on “settlements”. He *knew* that a settlement was five trailers in the middle of 100,000 Arabs. If only Israel would get rid of the “settlements”, the Arabs wouldn’t bother them anymore.

It’s pointless to call someone more or less of a Jew – that definition is binary. Still, criticism from outside of the Holy Land too often comes from people who do not have all of the facts. If they would live here and see the truth with their own eyes, they may feel differently.
