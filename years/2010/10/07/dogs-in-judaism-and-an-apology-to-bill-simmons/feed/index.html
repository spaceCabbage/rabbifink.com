However, I will note that while the dog was seen as a fairly base creature, it wasn’t viewed entirely negatively. The midrash relates that dogs received the mitzvah of neveilah for not barking at the Jews during yetzias mitzrayim. Cats didn’t really exist so much in ancient Israel (probably because it reminded people of their Egyptian overlords) but they did have dogs which were utilized as farm animals helping in the ancient Israelite tradition of shepherding, if not pets outright. A feral dog is a dangerous one, but I doubt that domesticated dogs were ever counted amongst the dangerous animals (anymore so than a tam ox).
