Regret can become negative. Regret that is not dealt with appropriately can morph into guilt and be harmful.

Rambam in his description of the Teshuva (repentance) process lists regret (charata) as one of the main ingredients of that process.

Using regret as part of repentance is a great way to use regret. The section of the essay that uses regret negatively is outside the framework of repentance. It was used as support for the teshuva ideal.
