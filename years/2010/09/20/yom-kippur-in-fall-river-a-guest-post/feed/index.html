Adas I Wed, 22 Sep 2010 20:31:00 +0000 http://finkorswim.com/?p=2861#comment-3215 As Pres. of the shul, it fell on me to try to make sure a minyan would be present for Yom Kippur, after all, the Chazan was coming from NJ for the 4th year in a row, and I felt it my duty to guarantee a minyan. I found out the day before that Shimon Pepper was coming, and after all he is my wife’s first cousin, I had to make an extra effort. To my delight and surprise we not only had a minyan for Kol Nidre, but all the way through Neila and Maariv after Shabbos. What a pleasure it was to see some semblance of the “old days”, when if you got there 1 minute late, you had to stand in the back of the auditorium because there were no seats. Shimon’s personal account of his experience, left me with a renewed hope that maybe everything would be all right I am forever the eternal optimist, and am not afraid of hard work, so if that is what it takes, as the kids say today “bring it on”. Our shul is open to all who would like to come and worship, and to all we say “welcome !!”
Jeffrey Weissman, September 2010
