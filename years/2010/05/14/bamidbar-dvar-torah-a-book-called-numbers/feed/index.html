Fri, 14 May 2010 19:39:03 +0000 http://finkorswim.com/?p=2458#comment-2064 Forgive me for mentioning a personal bugaboo (or maybe ba’alei k’riah share this) but the word in the first passuk from which the sefer takes its name is B’midbar or Bemidbar with a sh’va na on the bet, not Bamidbar with a patach. Grammatically, that is because it is a s’michut (genitive) connecting to the next word Sinai. This never causes problems with the name of the second Chumash (also a genitive form.) No one ever calls that Chumash Shaymot.
