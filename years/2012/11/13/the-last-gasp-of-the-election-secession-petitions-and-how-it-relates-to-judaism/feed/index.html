]]> By: Shlomo Pill http://finkorswim.com/2012/11/13/the-last-gasp-of-the-election-secession-petitions-and-how-it-relates-to-judaism/#comment-10899 Tue, 13 Nov 2012 18:41:00 +0000 http://finkorswim.com/?p=6465#comment-10899 Of course, the famous disagreement between Rabbis Hirsch and Bamberger over Torah-observant Jews’ duty to secede from official institutional communities controlled by Reform Jews (since Reform Jews constituted a significant majority of the Jewish communal population at the time). There are good arguments on both sides of the issue, but it boils down to two issues: (1) How do we distinguish between legitimate disagreement with Jews who do things differently than we do and conduct that sets other Jews completely outside the pale of rabbinic Judaism; (2) What does association with an institutional organization mean – does it indicate (tacit) approval of that institution’s policies and the views that underlie them, or does it amount to nothing more than a matter of convenience and a need to have your own means met through association with a powerful organization that can meet those needs (even it it acts in ways diametrically opposed to your most fundamental principles).

As applied to these petitions, or to the secession movement generally (yes, it is a movement), the issues similarly boil down to (1) How do we distinguish between legitimate disagreement about what the constitution and Constitution of the United States requires/allows, and between clear and illegitimate violations of the basic legitimizing fabric of the nation; and (2) Assuming the latter characterized the current state of the Federal government, what is the appropriate response.

These petitions are admittedly a rather juvenile way of venting anger about the results of last week’s elections. But for those more thoughtful on the issue, they (wrongly, in my view) view President Obama’s reelection as yet another symbol of the Federal government rejection of what they see as acceptable bounds for constitutional government, and more importantly as a sign that so many of the people of the several States with which they are associated are not bothered by (what they view as) the illegitimate violation of Constitutional norms.
