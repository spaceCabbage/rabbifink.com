]]> By: E. Fink http://finkorswim.com/2012/03/07/hesped-for-rabbi-yosef-tendler-zatzal/#comment-7399 E. Fink Fri, 09 Mar 2012 20:13:00 +0000 http://finkorswim.com/?p=5248#comment-7399 Thank you. I appreciate it.

To further clarify daas Torah: There is a new version of daas Torah that is not what we being referred to by Rabbi Tendler. I know he was not referring to the new version because we talked many times about the concept.

The new version holds that there a select few people who possess daas Torah. It is akin to nevuah, sometimes it is called ruach hakodesh, and it implies that the one who speaks with daas Torah cannot be mistaken about what he is speaking.

Rabbi Tendler, and all my rebbeim in Ner Yisrael believed that studying Torah with intensity had the ability to help mold one’s mind into a mind that could think in the way that the Torah “thinks”. This kind of daas Torah is accessible to anyone who learns well and more importantly does not imply that the one speaking is incapable of being mistaken.

You asked: “Is rav elyashiv and rav chaim kanievsky daas torah?”

A person cannot “be” daas Torah. Using the “old” definition, these great Torah scholars certainly possess a keen intellect that is very useful and helpful when one needs advice. But it does not make them incapable of error, nor does it mean that they will be helpful in situations where they have no expertise. It also does not mean that if they make a proclamation one is obligated to keep their proclamation.

(For further reading see my short essay here: http://dovbear.blogspot.com/2010/12/in-defense-of-emunas-chachamim-and-daas.html)
