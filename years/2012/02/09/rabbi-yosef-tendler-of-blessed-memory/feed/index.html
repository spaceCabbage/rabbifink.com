]]> By: a student http://finkorswim.com/2012/02/09/rabbi-yosef-tendler-of-blessed-memory/#comment-7294 a student Fri, 02 Mar 2012 07:47:00 +0000 http://finkorswim.com/?p=5133#comment-7294 He was a great man. I still find myself quoting him, or one of his anecdotes on a regular basis. There is one story that stands out in my mind right now and i have no idea why but ill share it. Rav tendler gave a bekius shiur to a select group of 12th graders (i have no idea what his selection was based on though) in which i had the privilege of being in. once, when we were in middle of shiur, the freshmen were making a racket in the hallway and acting very childishly. He had a look of pride on his face as he looked upon us, the seniors, and said “they are just babies, they will grow up, you were babies too” then he froze for a good few seconds, cleared his throat and gave his signature chuckle. He took great pride in his students. 
another time, around 8 years ago, i was learning in a beis hamidrash in brooklyn and i saw a high school aged student learning there as well. i struck him up in conversation, turns out he went to ner yisrael and was kicked out by rav tendler for a silly thing he did , mind you rav tendler hardly ever kicked kids out, he just asked them to leave and after a week or two the kid was back in yeshiva, any way, this kids in the beis hamidrash learning, (looking up a rav moshe if i recall), he told me that he was going back to yeshiva next week and then he proceeded to complain about how rav tendler kicked him out and how he doesnt know what he is doing and yadayadayada. after a while i asked the kid “what are you doing here in the beis right now, you dont have school for a week, go party!” the kid looked at me and said “that would be such a waste of a day” . i guess rav tendler really did know what he was doing.
