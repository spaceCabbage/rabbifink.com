]]> By: Arthur http://finkorswim.com/2012/05/23/new-koren-english-talmud/#comment-8807 Thu, 24 May 2012 22:01:00 +0000 http://finkorswim.com/?p=5617#comment-8807 Rabbi Fink,

First, thank you for your thought-provoking essays.

I find it surprising that you and the many other reviewers completely fail
to mention that this is the second attempt to present the Steinsaltz Talmud to
the English-speaking public. The first effort, backed by the marketing muscle
of publishing powerhouse Random House. The work was a dismal failure, despite
many strong efforts to promote it for a number of years.

As for the rest of your observations, the Steinsaltz Talmud was not banned
because it failed to adhere to the “tzuras hadaf” (the Vilna page
layout), though that was one reason it was not widely accepted in the classic
yeshivos.

A far more fundamental reason was, as Leon Wieseltier put it in a 1989 New
York Times review (http://www.nytimes.com/1989/12/17/books/unlocking-the-rabbis-secrets.html?scp=1&sq=leon%20wieseltier%20secrets&st=cse),
the Steinsaltz Talmud is for “studying,” not “learning.”
The Steinsaltz treatment is informative, but if fails to give you the true experience
of being engrossed in the gemara.

The ban on Rabbi Steinsaltz was related to another issue, one touched upon very
directly by the promotional video clip.

The ban was based on Rabbi Steinsaltz, throughout his works, failing to
treat the heroes of Scripture and Talmud with respect and reverence.

Placed in that context, that video teaser isn’t merely ridiculous, it is a
reflection of a weltanschauung that should give people serious about religion
and religious traditions serious pause.

I’ve seen copies of the Talmud from before them with the same layout. They may have finalized and touched up the layout, and perhaps organized the commentaries on the side a bit, but the Gemara, Rashi, and Tosafos were already set up.
