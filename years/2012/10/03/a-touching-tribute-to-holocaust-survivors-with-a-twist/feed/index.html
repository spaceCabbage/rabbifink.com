]]> By: Neil Weinberg http://finkorswim.com/2012/10/03/a-touching-tribute-to-holocaust-survivors-with-a-twist/#comment-10486 Wed, 10 Oct 2012 04:03:00 +0000 http://finkorswim.com/?p=6296#comment-10486 The Nazis used these tattoos to dehumanize Jews—we were not people with names, families, or personal identities, but chattel to be catalogued, shipped off to camps, and in millions of cases exterminated. Although the grandchildren in the NYT article clearly wish to honor their grandparents suffering, it is difficult for me to see how a symbol of such brutality could ever be re-appropriated.

A similar attempt at coöptation in the US provides a somewhat cautionary analogy. In recent decades, some African-Americans have chosen to refer to one another by the N-word in the belief that this will simultaneously evoke the memory of racism and emasculate it. Yet, the majority of Americans (black, white and otherwise) still find the term extremely offensive. As the discipline of semiotics has taught us, the meanings of language, signs and symbols are not so easily malleable.
