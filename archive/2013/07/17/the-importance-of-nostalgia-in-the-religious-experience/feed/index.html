I’m a BT, but I still have a lifetime of positive Jewish nostalgia.

My family did shabbat dinner, Pesach seders, going to Reform Temple (not every week, but more than twice a year); and of course, Chanukah.

I have a lifelong positive Jewish identity. So, once I decided to seek out Truth, I went to Jews who claimed to know Truth first. (Reform doesn’t even bother claiming that their practices are the True Will of Hashem).

Then again, I know many BT’s and gerim, who had no Jewish content in their childhoods, who are very happy shomrei mitzvot today.

So, nostalgia can help someone take on / keep their mitzvah observance, but I don’t think it’s as strong of a factor as a supportive spouse, family, and community in the present.
