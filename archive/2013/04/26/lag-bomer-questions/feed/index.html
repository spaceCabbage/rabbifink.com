I would like to propose an alternate name: Chadash Assur Min HaTorah Day.

I think it just has so much more nuance. It reflects the original contextual mean of Chadash that we’re supposed to be counting the days until Chadash becomes Yasahan after the Karban Omer, a concept that has largely been relegated to esoterica in the Diaspora. It’s evidence that the halachic system evolves and allows for changes toward leniency when needed. Lag BaOmer itself, as a day of any significance, along with the accompanying mourning period is itself a time honored innovation. It’s evidence that the Chasam Sofer novella is wrong. The even more recent RaShbY centric customs are further evidence that innovation is embraced by all sorts when it speaks to them. It’s evidence that even that which may be shtus, and rooted in chukot hagoyim is sometime incorporated and that just because the innovator is wearing a streimel doesn’t make it correct.
