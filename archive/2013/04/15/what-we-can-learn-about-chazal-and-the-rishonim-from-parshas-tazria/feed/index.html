Specifically you asked if the idea that women prefer to marry is Divine. I don’t think so and I see no reason to believe it is beneficial to accept that it is Divine.

I greatly prefer fallible gedolim who erred in matters of medicine than the alternative – I am not even sure how one would articulate the alternative, perhaps “siding with Chazal and Rishonim over science and medicine” – I’m not sure. But either way, this is good for my emunah and the emunah of many others. I think it is a great principle that we should be proud to proclaim that our great rabbis used ideas contemporary to their times to interpret the Torah. I think that makes their insight even greater.
