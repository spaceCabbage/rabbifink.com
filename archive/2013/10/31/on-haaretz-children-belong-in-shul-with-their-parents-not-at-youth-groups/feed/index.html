]]> By: Richard Polirer http://finkorswim.com/2013/10/31/on-haaretz-children-belong-in-shul-with-their-parents-not-at-youth-groups/#comment-14543 Fri, 01 Nov 2013 00:23:00 +0000 http://finkorswim.com/?p=7772#comment-14543 I attended the Young Israel of Brownsville and East Flatbush when I was young. We had youth services, grades 3-8, during the regular prayers. There, we learned how to lead services, read Torah and Haftorah, and even give a d’var Torah. After Services, we joined the regular congregation for the closing hymns, which WE led. When I entered YU HS, we had a teenage minyan, as well. And every few months, we took over in the main sanctuary and led and read and chanted and sermonized. It was a hothouse of Judaism for a brief moment — some 6 years — of my life, 1962-68. No other shul I ever davvened in had that kind of atmosphere for the younger generation, sad to say. But THOSE years? They were geshmack!

Of course, that is nostalgia — nostos algos in Greek, meaning “the sickness of homecoming, memory.” Maybe things are different today — everything else has changed, so why not youth groups. Rav Fink, let us see the FULL article, so we can know fully whereof you speak, ok? Kol Tuv.
