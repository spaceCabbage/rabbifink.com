]]> By: John Lucsoft http://finkorswim.com/2012/04/26/my-uncle-ronnie-greenwald-speaks-at-the-agudah-convention/#comment-7963 Mon, 30 Apr 2012 01:46:00 +0000 http://finkorswim.com/?p=5445#comment-7963 I can’t decide what I think about this. On the one hand, I commend him for standing up and saying his piece, even when it’s not exactly the party line. He is blunt and honest, which is great. I’m proud to be related :-).

On the other hand, it bothers me that he – like many others in the religious world – conflates the “kids at risk” issue with religious defection. What exactly does he mean when he says “challenged”? Is that synonymous with the general definition of “kids at risk” (e.g., http://nyyouthatrisk.org/) or does that mean kids who do not accept orthodox Judaism? Or both?

The two are completely distinct issues. Yes, they often overlap, but they don’t have to. And while I recognize the need for (and support) the work he’s doing for kids at risk, I do not care much for kiruv. 

I think that many in the orthodox world try to pretend that no one leaves orthodoxy unless they are unstable, mentally ill, on drugs, or stupid. This is patently false, and it bothers me. I believe it is simply a way of convincing themselves and their children that there is no good reason to leave. After all, if what you have is obviously so much better, anyone who disagrees must have some serious issues.
