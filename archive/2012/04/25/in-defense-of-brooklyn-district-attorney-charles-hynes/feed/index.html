]]> By: Elliot Pasik, Esq. http://finkorswim.com/2012/04/25/in-defense-of-brooklyn-district-attorney-charles-hynes/#comment-8023 Wed, 02 May 2012 12:51:00 +0000 http://finkorswim.com/?p=5428#comment-8023 The Brooklyn D.A. is not a one-man legislature.  Statutory law gets made by the two houses of the democratically elected NYS Legislature in Albany – the 150 member Assembly, and the 62 member Senate.  A bill gets passed, and the Governor signs it, or vetoes.  The D.A.’s thin basis for not disclosing the names of arrested orthodox sex crime defendants is NY Civil Rights Law section 50-b(1) which states as follows:  

“§ 50-b. Right of privacy; victims of sex offenses or offenses involving the transmission of the human immunodeficiency virus. 1. The identity of any victim of a sex offense, as defined in article one
hundred thirty or section 255.25, 255.26 or 255.27 of the penal law, or
of an offense involving the alleged transmission of the human
immunodeficiency virus, shall be confidential. No report, paper,
picture, photograph, court file or other documents, in the custody or
possession of any public officer or employee, which identifies such a
victim shall be made available for public inspection. No such public
officer or employee shall disclose any portion of any police report,
court file, or other document, which tends to identify such a victim
except as provided in subdivision two of this section.”This statute is utterly silent about keeping the names of arrested defendants confidential. The D.A. has no legal authority to add language to the statute that does not exist, nor to give the statute a meaning that was never remotely contemplated by the state legislature when this law, then a bill, was first proposed, debated, passed, andsigned into law by the Governor. Additionally, not one commentator has pointed to any other D.A., in our nation, that has ever acted in a similar fashion. This Brooklyn D.A. has, thus, usurped normal democratic powers reserved for the legislature and the voters.Rabbi Fink, you may feel that the Brooklyn D.A. is right, notwithstanding that he and other D.A.s can begin to withhold the names of other sex crime defendants who originate from other religious and ethnic communities, notwithstanding no legal basis to do so. The difficulty with your point of view is that it is totally non-democratic and authoritarian, and that is one of the underlying reasons why our community has this sex abuse problem in the first place.
