]]> By: Anonymous http://finkorswim.com/2012/06/20/answering-the-difficult-questions/#comment-9628 Fri, 22 Jun 2012 11:49:00 +0000 http://finkorswim.com/?p=5749#comment-9628 I don’t think think is an issue that has an “answer.” Every child has different reasons for asking questions and therefore should be answered differently. Isn’t this the message of the four sons?

As a teacher, I have always found that the most effective manner of dealing with the modern sensibilities question is to validate multiple opinions. There are Orthodox thinkers who approve of certain adaptations, and that adapting the Torah to modem values is not automatically heresy. Additionally, there are those who believe in no change at all, and their opinions are rooted in sources as well.

The most important thing I can convey to my students is that whatever approach they are following, they should make sure they are being honest with themselves, and that they have people who can help them think through their opinions to maintain self-honesty.

Finally, we have to have patience. If a child of four beliefs Vashti had a tail, it is not the end of the world. You have plenty of time to correct that option (if you feel the need) when they are older. The key is to give them what they can handle based on their developmental level and to show respect for their opinions.
