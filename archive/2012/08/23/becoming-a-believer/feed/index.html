]]> By: Menachem Lipkin http://finkorswim.com/2012/08/23/becoming-a-believer/#comment-10238 Fri, 24 Aug 2012 04:50:00 +0000 http://finkorswim.com/?p=6152#comment-10238 Is the “value” in believing or in being a believer? Not sure there is much intrinsic value to believing, but there is enormous reinforcement in being part of a believer community. As part of “our” community you:

– are told that, as a Jew, you’re pretty much the center of the universe, the reason for creation.
– are given the security of “knowing” that after you die you’ll go to a better place. (You just have to follow the rules.)
– have the comfort of knowing that you’ll see all of your dead loved ones again someday.
– are strongly reinforced for all of your religious actions; praying, learning, keeping Kosher and Shabbat etc.
– have a strong support system of hundreds, if not thousands, for employment, care when you’re ill, finding housing, etc.
– And much more…

With all of these psychological and physical benefits it is easy to see how people can suspend critical thinking in order to belong and how they’ll let their minds be “convinced” by weak “evidence” like blurry pictures, hashgacha pratit stories, or shoddy “God of the gaps” logic.

The challenge is to be able synthesize rational thought with belief. And as your previous post pointed out, that is becoming increasingly difficult in our day and age. Mr. Edwards and your Chabbad lady may actually explain why so many believers choose not to engage in critical thinking – the benefits of being a believer may actually outweigh rational thinking for some.
