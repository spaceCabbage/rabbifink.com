But beyond the misogynistic statements about women in our tradition – things that clearly offend our modern sensibilities – what about the halachic structures that appear to relegate women to second-class status, such as Edut, Kiddushin/Gerushin/Aguna, Minyan, Tzniyut, Kol Isha, etc.? If you haven’t yet done so, I recommend Rabbi David Hartman’s “The God Who Hates Lies.” He has a chapter about this very issue.

Also, one might say that it is modern, western life that has had the greatest impact on women’s status in Orthodox Judaism. But the Haredi world, by and large, rejects much, if not all, of this influence. The “Torani/Haredi Leumi” world does as well.

Is it a surprise then, when some segments of these communities elevate in importance those rabbinic statements and positions that seem to run most counter to modern views of women? Since being reactionary is part of their hashkafa, the advancement of women in contemporary society drives them toward the more retrogade elements of the Jewish tradition.
