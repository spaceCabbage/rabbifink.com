    Michael Vick has admitted what he did to dogs was wrong and has sought to amend his ways.  Have we admitted our own complicity here and sought to improve our ways?  Factory farming causes far more animal suffering than dog fighting, the fur industry, or the Canadian seal hunt; moreover, it also oppresses many of the low wage, often immigrant workers doing the dirty work.  We dare not turn a blind eye.

    We should not have to choose between kashrut and Jewish ethics. Torah embraces both of them. K’doshim tihiyu–You shall be holy–to which Ramban adds that one should not be a naval b’reshut haTorah.  At every meal we make a choice.  We can and must live–and eat–to a higher standard.

This misunderstands the Reform mindset on this. Most Reform Jews couldn’t care less about kashrut. I’m certain that there are some on kashrut. And I’m certain that the gist is that keep kosher is cool if you’re into it, but if you don’t wanna, you eat what you want.

There are a number of Reform shuls (the far predate this stuff) that have kosher kitchens. And the rabbi at the Reform shul I grew up at keeps kosher.
