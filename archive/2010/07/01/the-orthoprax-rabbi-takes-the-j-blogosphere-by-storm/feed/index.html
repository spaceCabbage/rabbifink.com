]]> By: Prof K http://finkorswim.com/2010/07/01/the-orthoprax-rabbi-takes-the-j-blogosphere-by-storm/#comment-2249 Sun, 04 Jul 2010 18:47:00 +0000 http://finkorswim.com/?p=2582#comment-2249 Religions are generally belief systems in a superior form–in the case of the Jews, God. Therefore, any of those various divisions of Judaism are belief based. Certainly orthodoxy can be said to be constructed this way. So how is it possible to say that one can be orthodox–a label that can only be applied to those who are “true and full” believers of a God-based religion–but not believe in God? Conflict of terms here. “Real” practice of a religion requires, at a minimum, a belief in the underlying system. Otherwise a person is not orthodox, or orthoprax if you must, but is merely aping some outward practices of the orthodox Jews. If I eat fish on Friday and attend mass does that make me Catholic? The catholics would immediately say no. Why are we so reticent to say that whatever the “prax” people are doing, it’s not a Jewish practice, much less an orthodox practice.
