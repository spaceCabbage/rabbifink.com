Those who claim not to believe in G-d, and yet continue to act according to Biblical morality most of the time
(even when not bound to do so by secular law), are lying to themselves.

They obviously recognize that there is a Creator.

They simply fool themselves into thinking otherwise, so as to justify a few practices that are either
socially convenient or for which they have an overwhelming physical desire.

If there is no (seemingly) insurmountable obstacle, such as social pressure or great physical desire,
the “moral atheist” will follow G-d’s laws, even when there is gain to be had from violation.

In the specific case of religiously educated Jews, this is why a Jewish “atheist” will often 
refrain from improper (but legal) business practices.   He will likely do large favors for others, and give good amounts of charity (even though it causes him a loss of time and resources).

 In fact, this “atheist” will fulfill EVEN MORE of the commandments that he doesn’t have a strong societal or physical desire to break! 

 All of this is illogical. If there is no G-d, then just follow secular law. “Good” and “evil” are definitions given to actions by the Torah.   If the Torah is work of fiction, then the labels of “good” on charity and “evil” on taking revenge are fictional as well.

Since we find that the vast majority of self-avowed Jewish atheists continue to follow many mitzvot, we must conclude that they secretly still  know and understand that “Hashem Hu Elo-heem”.
