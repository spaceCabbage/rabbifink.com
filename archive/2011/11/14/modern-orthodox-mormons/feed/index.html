Before that time, I was quite worldly. Today, I work for Hewlett Packard and see no problem fitting into society. Certain social activities may be an acception. For example, I tend to avoid activities where people are wearing immodest clothes, drinking large amounts of alchohol, using crude language or watching movies that are beneath my standards.

Other than obvious things such as not drinking, smoking, swearing, etc., I feel like I fit into society better than many. In many ways, the Mormon church is on the cutting edge of society. The church promotes education, good careers, family life as equals between men and women, being involved in governent and the community and a balanced life of service and wholesome recreational activities. The church is down on family abuse and unhealthy practices. The church is a mix of modernity mixed with down home traditional values.

Having grown up in a heavily protestant culture (I have ministers in my family), Mormonism feels like the cutting edge of religion. The leaders of the Mormon church from the bottom to the top are not folks with religious degrees. They were called out of society. Many of the top leaders were called out of lucrative positions to serve the rest of their lives in the church where there is no paid clergy. The quorum of the 12 apostles includes a state supremene court judge, a famous sergeon, a prominant physicist, 2 college presidents, and some leaders in industry.

The church uses  practical and professional practices to take the gospel of Jesus Christ to the world. To me, it is the cadillac of Christinity.
