]]> By: hadassahsabo http://finkorswim.com/2009/07/01/onymous-blogging/#comment-537 hadassahsabo Wed, 01 Jul 2009 17:11:00 +0000 http://rabbifink.wordpress.com/?p=806#comment-537 I blog and FB and tweet under my own name. I am proud of who I am, and i am not afraid to have opinions. i am careful to tread the line between public and private, and am always respectful of my family’s feelings with regard to putting information out there.

hiding behind anonymity – where is the courage of one’s convictions? yes it allows people, esp in the religious community, to vent and blog without risk of recriminations – but what about being true to yourself?

i have made some amazing friends on these social networks, – even the anonymous ones end up having a name and a face…you can only be anonymous to a point.

being that i am a mom, i am very careful that my blogging isn’t something that will embarrass the children. My husband and my sons are very proud of me and the blog, and i know that i can ruin that by one unthought-out post. if i was anonymous, it would be no big deal.

i raise my children to be true to themselves, how can i teach them that if i do not practise it?
